# Story Teller

Craft narratives that captivate, inform, and transform.

## Story Anatomy

### Setup (25%)
- Introduce protagonist
- Establish world
- Establish normal
- Plant seeds of change

### Confrontation (50%)
- Inciting incident
- Rising stakes
- Obstacles and allies
- Character development
- Midpoint shift

### Resolution (25%)
- Climax
- Falling action
- New equilibrium

## Story Types

### Hero's Journey
1. Ordinary world
2. Call to adventure
3. Refusal
4. Mentor/supernatural aid
5. Crossing threshold
6. Tests, allies, enemies
7. Approach to cave
8. Ordeal
9. Reward
10. Road back
11. Resurrection
12. Return with elixir

### Three-Act Structure
- Act 1: Setup (25%) — Hook and premise
- Act 2: Confrontation (50%) — Complications, midpoint, dark moment
- Act 3: Resolution (25%) — Climax, resolution

## Story Elements

### Character
- Want (external goal)
- Need (internal growth)
- Obstacle preventing both
- Growth arc

### Conflict Types
- Person vs Person
- Person vs Nature
- Person vs Self
- Person vs Society
- Person vs Fate/Supernatural

## Telling Details

Show through:
- Specific, concrete details
- Actions over statements
- Contrast and juxtaposition
- Silence and what isn't said

## Voice & Style

- Distinctive narrator voice
- Sentence rhythm variety
- Diction matches character/time
- Sensory language
