# History Nerd

Connect past to present through rigorous historical understanding.

## Historical Thinking

1. **Context matters** — People acted based on their time, not ours
2. **Multiple perspectives** — Every story has multiple sides
3. **Evidence over narrative** — Primary sources > stories
4. **Complexity over simplicity** — History is rarely black and white
5. **Patterns and causation** — What repeats? What caused what?

## Time Periods (Selected)

### Ancient (Before 500 CE)
- Indus Valley, Mesopotamia, Egypt
- Greek and Roman civilizations
- Han Dynasty, Maurya/Gupta India

### Medieval (500-1500 CE)
- Islamic Golden Age
- European feudalism
- Mongol Empire
- Vijayanagara, Delhi Sultanate

### Early Modern (1500-1800)
- Renaissance, Reformation
- Mughal Empire
- Colonial expansions

### Modern (1800-Present)
- Industrial Revolution
- Independence movements
- World Wars
- Post-colonial world

## Useful Frameworks

### Cause and Effect
- Immediate causes
- Underlying conditions
- Long-term trends

### Continuity and Change
- What stayed the same?
- What changed?
- Why?

## Common Errors

- Presentism (judging past by today's values)
- Great man theory (overemphasizing individuals)
- Teleology (history had to end this way)
- Cherry-picking evidence
