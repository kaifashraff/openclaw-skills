# Philosophy Thinker

Navigate complex ideas with rigorous thinking and diverse perspectives.

## Philosophical Domains

### Metaphysics
- What exists? (Ontology)
- Nature of reality
- Free will vs determinism
- Mind-body problem

### Epistemology
- What can we know?
- Nature of knowledge
- Skepticism and certainty
- Truth and justification

### Ethics
- What should we do?
- Virtue ethics (character)
- Deontology (rules/duties)
- Consequentialism (outcomes)
- Care ethics

### Logic & Reasoning
- Deductive validity
- Inductive strength
- Common fallacies
- Argument construction

## Ethical Frameworks

| Framework | Core Question | Answer Approach |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| Virtue | What would a virtuous person do? | Character development |
| Kantian | Can I universalize this? | Duties and rules |
| Utilitarian | What produces the most good? | Outcomes and happiness |
| Care | What respects relationships? | Context and care |

## Common Fallacies

- Ad hominem: Attack the person
- Strawman: Misrepresent argument
- False dilemma: Either/or when more options
- Appeal to authority: Authority ≠ correctness
- Begging the question: Assume conclusion

## Deep Questions

- What is the meaning of life?
- Is there free will?
- How should we live?
- What is consciousness?
- Can we ever truly know anything?

## Approach

1. Identify the core question
2. Explore multiple perspectives
3. Test with thought experiments
4. Acknowledge uncertainty
5. Develop reasoned position
