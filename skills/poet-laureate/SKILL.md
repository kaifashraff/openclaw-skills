# Poet Laureate

Craft with words — precise, evocative, alive.

## Poetry Fundamentals

### Sound
- Rhythm: Pattern of stressed/unstressed syllables
- Meter: Regular rhythm (iambic, trochaic)
- Rhyme: End sounds (perfect, slant, internal)
- Alliteration: Repeated initial sounds
- Assonance: Repeated vowel sounds
- Consonance: Repeated consonant sounds

### Imagery
- Visual: What we see
- Auditory: What we hear
- Kinesthetic: Movement, touch
- Olfactory/Gustatory: Smell/taste

### Figures of Speech
- Metaphor: X is Y (implied comparison)
- Simile: X is like Y (explicit comparison)
- Personification: Human traits to non-human
- Hyperbole: Exaggeration
- Metonymy: Associated for actual
- Synecdoche: Part for whole

## Forms

### Sonnet (14 lines)
- Shakespearean: 3 quatrains + couplet, abab cdcd efef gg
- Petrarchan: Octave + sestet, abbaabba cdecde

### Free Verse
- No fixed meter or rhyme
- Line breaks create rhythm
- Still has internal logic

### Haiku (Japanese)
- 3 lines, 5-7-5 syllables
- Nature/seasonal focus
- Image + insight

### Ghazal (Urdu/Arabic)
- 5-7 couplets
- First couplet sets rhyme scheme
- Each couplet complete in itself

## Poetic Process

1. **Seed** — Image, feeling, phrase
2. **Draft** — Let flow, don't judge
3. **Cut** — Remove what's not essential
4. **Music** — Read aloud, hear the rhythm
5. **Refine** — Every word earns its place

## Poetic Modes

**Narrative:** Tells a story
**Lyric:** Expresses emotion
**Dramatic:** Character voice
**Confessional:** Personal experience

## Advice

- Show, don't tell
- Concrete images over abstract statements
- White space is part of poem
- End with resonance, not summary
