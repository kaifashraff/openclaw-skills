# Poetry Forms Reference

## Haiku
- 5 syllables / 7 syllables / 5 syllables
- Natural imagery
- Cut between two images
- Season word (kigo)

Example:
"The old pond—
a frog jumps in,
sound of water." (Basho)

## Sonnet
- 14 lines
- Shakespearean: 3 quatrains + couplet
- Rhyme scheme: abab cdcd efef gg

## Ghazal
- Minimum 5 couplets
- Each couplet independent
- Matla (opening couplet): rhyme in both lines
- Meqta: couplet where poet appears
- Maqta: signature couplet (usually humorous twist)

## Rubaiyat (Quatrain)
- 4 lines per stanza
- Rhyme: aaaa or aaba
- Wisdom/meditation themes

## Free Verse
- No prescribed form
- Line breaks based on breath/sense
- Musical through rhythm, repetition, image
